# Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 6, 2026
Version: privacy_v1_0_0

This Privacy Policy is a draft for MarSci Diagnostics. It is intended to describe our current planned and partially implemented data practices in plain English. It is not final legal advice, and some sections may change as the product, infrastructure, and legal review process mature.

## 1. Who We Are

MarSci Diagnostics is a software-as-a-service platform for marketing diagnostics and related account-level analysis. MarSci Diagnostics is operated from Thailand.

## 3. Categories of Personal Data We Collect

Depending on how you use MarSci Diagnostics, we may collect:

- identity and account data, such as your name, email address, and profile image;
- authentication data from Google OAuth sign-in;
- account identifiers and internal user identifiers;
- billing references, such as Stripe customer identifiers, checkout references, subscription identifiers, invoice status, and payment status;
- usage data, such as request dates, platform/account identifiers, logs, and diagnostic usage history;
- diagnostic result history and related service output records;
- technical data, such as IP address, browser/device details, and timestamps; and
- support communications you send to us.

We do not intend to store full payment card details in our systems. Stripe handles payment card processing.

## 4. How We Collect Information

We may collect information:

- directly from you when you sign in, subscribe, contact support, or use the service;
- from Google when you use Google OAuth authentication;
- from Stripe when checkout, billing, subscription, or invoice events are sent to us;
- automatically through the application, logs, and operational tooling; and
- through cookies or similar technologies where applicable.

## 5. Lawful Basis for Processing (PDPA)

Where Thailand's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) applies, we may process personal data on one or more of the following bases:

- contract performance, such as providing account access, diagnostics, or subscription services;
- legitimate interests, such as service security, fraud prevention, troubleshooting, logging, and product improvement;
- consent, where optional features or optional communications require it; and
- legal obligations, such as accounting, tax, compliance, or lawful disclosure requirements.

The applicable basis may vary depending on the data and the context.

## 6. Purpose of Processing

We may use personal data to:

- authenticate users and manage account access;
- deliver diagnostics and related service functionality;
- administer subscriptions, billing, and payment operations;
- respond to support requests;
- maintain service security, auditability, and operational reliability;
- improve product quality and support operations; and
- comply with legal obligations where applicable.

## 7. Google OAuth / Account Authentication

MarSci Diagnostics uses Google OAuth for sign-in. When you sign in with Google, we may receive information such as your email address, name, profile image, and a Google account identifier that is used to link your Google account to your MarSci Diagnostics account.

We use this information to authenticate you, create or link your account, maintain access, and support account administration where available.

We do not state that Google endorses MarSci Diagnostics.

## 8. Billing and Payments via Stripe

Stripe provides checkout and billing services for MarSci Diagnostics. When you purchase a subscription, Stripe may collect and process payment information directly. We may receive limited billing-related data from Stripe, including:

- Stripe customer identifiers;
- checkout session references;
- subscription identifiers;
- invoice and payment status; and
- plan-related metadata.

We do not intend to store full card numbers, card security codes, or full payment instrument details in our own systems.

## 9. Cookies and Similar Technologies

MarSci Diagnostics may use cookies, local storage, or similar technologies for authentication, session handling, security, and service performance. Additional usage-measurement or service-monitoring tools, if added, may also use cookies or similar technologies.

The exact cookie and service-measurement setup may change over time. Where applicable, additional notices or controls may be provided when available.

## 10. Data Retention

We may retain data for different periods depending on the type of data and the purpose of processing. In general:

- account data may be retained while an account remains active and for a reasonable period afterward;
- billing and invoice-related data may be retained for accounting, tax, and audit purposes;
- security, diagnostic, and operational logs may be retained for service integrity, troubleshooting, and abuse prevention; and
- support records may be retained for follow-up, troubleshooting, and business record purposes.

Retention periods are not fully finalized and may vary by data type, legal obligation, and infrastructure changes.

## 11. Data Sharing and Service Providers

We may share personal data with service providers and infrastructure vendors that support the operation of MarSci Diagnostics, including providers for:

- authentication,
- billing,
- hosting,
- logging,
- service measurement,

We may also disclose information:

## 12. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Your personal data may be processed or stored outside Thailand, including by service providers such as Google, Stripe, and cloud infrastructure providers. The exact countries and transfer mechanisms may vary depending on the infrastructure and vendors in use.

Where applicable, we may take steps intended to support lawful cross-border transfers, but this section may need further review as infrastructure and legal requirements evolve.

## 13. Data Subject Rights (PDPA)

Subject to applicable law and operational limitations, you may have the right to:

- request access to your personal data;
- request correction or rectification of inaccurate data;
- request erasure, where applicable;
- request restriction of processing, where applicable;
- request data portability, where applicable;
- withdraw consent where processing depends on consent; and
- lodge a complaint with the Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC) of Thailand.

Some rights may be limited by law, technical feasibility, or legitimate business requirements.

Where we rely on consent, you may contact support@marsci-diagnostics.com to request withdrawal of consent. Withdrawal may affect the availability of certain optional features or services.

## 15. Security Statement

We use administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to help protect information. However, no method of transmission or storage is fully secure, and we do not guarantee absolute security.

Some controls are still evolving as the product matures.
