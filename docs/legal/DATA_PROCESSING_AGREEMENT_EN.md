# Data Processing Agreement

Last updated: March 6, 2026

Status: Prelaunch public legal pack

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is part of the prelaunch public legal pack for MarSci Diagnostics. It describes the intended public processing framework before general release and may require further review for specific customer relationships, jurisdictions, and infrastructure configurations.

## 1. Scope of Processing

MarSci Diagnostics may process personal data only to the extent reasonably necessary to provide the services requested by the Customer, including account administration, authentication, billing-related operations, platform connection support, diagnostic processing, and related support activities.

The processing is limited to the service relationship between the Customer and MarSci Diagnostics and is carried out on documented instructions from the Customer, except where processing is required by applicable law.

## 2. Types of Personal Data Processed

Depending on the Customer's use of the service, personal data processed may include:

- name
- email address
- account identifiers
- Google OAuth identity data
- Stripe customer references and related billing metadata
- platform or account integration metadata
- usage logs and support records
- marketing performance data that may include identifiers or account-linked metadata

MarSci Diagnostics does not intend to process full payment card details directly. Stripe handles payment card processing.

## 3. Categories of Data Subjects

Categories of data subjects may include:

- Customer users and administrators
- authorized Customer personnel
- platform account operators
- support contacts
- individuals whose identifiers may appear in account-linked marketing datasets, where applicable

## 4. Processor Obligations

MarSci Diagnostics shall:

- process personal data only for the agreed service purposes;
- not use Customer data for unrelated purposes except where permitted by law or contract;
- maintain reasonable confidentiality obligations for personnel and contractors with access to such data;
- assist the Customer, where reasonably possible, with requests relating to data subject rights;
- assist with compliance-related information requests where reasonably available; and
- notify the Customer if an instruction appears to violate applicable law, where appropriate and reasonably practicable.

## 5. Security Safeguards

MarSci Diagnostics uses administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to help protect personal data. These measures may include access controls, authentication controls, logging, limited role-based access, and service security practices appropriate to the nature of the service.

MarSci Diagnostics does not guarantee absolute security, and safeguards may evolve over time.

## 6. Sub-processors

MarSci Diagnostics may use sub-processors and service providers to operate the service, including providers for:

- hosting and cloud infrastructure
- authentication
- billing
- logging
- service measurement
- email delivery
- support tooling

Current or future sub-processors may include providers such as Google, Stripe, and cloud infrastructure vendors, where applicable. Further operational detail may be provided separately when available.

## 7. International Data Transfer

Customer data may be processed or stored outside Thailand or outside the Customer's own jurisdiction, depending on the location of infrastructure and service providers. MarSci Diagnostics may rely on service providers that process data across borders.

This section may require further legal review depending on customer location and actual hosting configuration.

## 8. Data Breach Notification

If MarSci Diagnostics becomes aware of a confirmed personal data breach affecting Customer data, it may notify the Customer without undue delay, taking into account the information reasonably available at the time and applicable legal obligations.

Initial notice may be limited and supplemented later as more information becomes available.

## 9. Data Deletion or Return on Termination

Upon termination of the applicable services, MarSci Diagnostics may delete or return Customer data where contractually required, operationally available, and legally permissible. Certain data may be retained for legal, accounting, audit, security, fraud prevention, or operational integrity reasons.

Deletion and return workflows are subject to actual technical implementation and legal constraints in effect at the relevant time.
