Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 6, 2026 Version privacy_v1_0_0 Operated from Thailand

This Privacy Policy describes our current planned and partially implemented data practices. It is a draft intended to reflect our operations and PDPA obligations. Some sections may change as the product matures. Governed by the laws of Thailand.

1

Who We Are

MarSci Diagnostics is a software-as-a-service platform for marketing diagnostics and related account-level analysis. MarSci Diagnostics is operated from Thailand.

2

Data Controller

MarSci Diagnostics
Operated from Thailand

  • General & support: support@marsci-diagnostics.com
  • Admin: admin@marsci-diagnostics.com
3

Categories of Personal Data We Collect

Depending on how you use MarSci Diagnostics, we may collect:

  • Identity & account data — name, email address, and profile image
  • Authentication data — from Google OAuth sign-in
  • Account identifiers — internal user identifiers
  • Billing references — Stripe customer identifiers, checkout references, subscription identifiers, invoice status, and payment status
  • Usage data — request dates, platform/account identifiers, logs, and diagnostic usage history
  • Diagnostic result history — service output records
  • Technical data — IP address, browser/device details, and timestamps
  • Support communications — messages you send to us
ℹ

We do not store full payment card details. Stripe handles payment card processing directly.

4

How We Collect Information

  • Directly from you when you sign in, subscribe, contact support, or use the service
  • From Google when you use Google OAuth authentication
  • From Stripe when checkout, billing, subscription, or invoice events are sent to us
  • Automatically through the application, logs, and operational tooling
  • Through cookies or similar technologies where applicable
5

Lawful Basis for Processing (PDPA)

Where Thailand's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) applies, we may process personal data on one or more of the following bases:

PDPA Legal Bases
  • Contract performance — providing account access, diagnostics, or subscription services
  • Legitimate interests — service security, fraud prevention, troubleshooting, logging, and product improvement
  • Consent — where optional features or communications require it
  • Legal obligations — accounting, tax, compliance, or lawful disclosure requirements
6

Purpose of Processing

  • Authenticate users and manage account access
  • Deliver diagnostics and related service functionality
  • Administer subscriptions, billing, and payment operations
  • Respond to support requests
  • Maintain service security, auditability, and operational reliability
  • Improve product quality and support operations
  • Comply with legal obligations where applicable
7

Google OAuth / Account Authentication

MarSci Diagnostics uses Google OAuth for sign-in. When you sign in with Google, we may receive your email address, name, profile image, and a Google account identifier used to link your Google account to your MarSci Diagnostics account.

We use this information to authenticate you, create or link your account, and support account administration. We do not claim that Google endorses MarSci Diagnostics.

8

Billing and Payments via Stripe

Stripe provides checkout and billing services. When you purchase a subscription, Stripe collects and processes payment information directly. We may receive limited billing-related data from Stripe:

  • Stripe customer identifiers
  • Checkout session references
  • Subscription identifiers
  • Invoice and payment status
  • Plan-related metadata

We do not store full card numbers, card security codes, or full payment instrument details in our own systems.

9

Cookies and Analytics

MarSci Diagnostics may use cookies, local storage, or similar technologies for authentication, session handling, security, and service performance. Analytics tooling, if added, may also use cookies or similar technologies.

The exact analytics and cookie setup may change over time. See our Cookie Policy for full details.

10

Data Retention

  • Account data — retained while an account is active and for a reasonable period afterward
  • Billing & invoice data — retained for accounting, tax, and audit purposes
  • Security & operational logs — retained for service integrity, troubleshooting, and abuse prevention
  • Support records — retained for follow-up, troubleshooting, and business record purposes

Retention periods may vary by data type, legal obligation, and infrastructure. See our Data Retention Policy.

11

Data Sharing and Service Providers

We may share personal data with service providers that support the operation of MarSci Diagnostics, including providers for:

  • Authentication, billing, hosting, logging, analytics, support, and email delivery

We may also disclose information: with your direction, to comply with legal obligations, to protect rights, safety, or security, or as part of a corporate transaction. See our Subprocessors list.

12

Cross-Border Data Transfers

Your personal data may be processed or stored outside Thailand, including by service providers such as Google, Stripe, and cloud infrastructure providers. The exact countries and transfer mechanisms may vary depending on the infrastructure and vendors in use.

Where applicable, we take steps intended to support lawful cross-border transfers under the PDPA framework.

13

Data Subject Rights (PDPA)

Subject to applicable law and operational limitations, you may have the right to:

Access

Request access to your personal data.

Correction

Request correction of inaccurate data.

Erasure

Request erasure where applicable.

Restriction

Request restriction of processing.

Portability

Request data portability where applicable.

Withdraw Consent

Withdraw consent where processing depends on it.

ℹ

You may also lodge a complaint with the Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC) of Thailand. Some rights may be limited by law, technical feasibility, or legitimate business requirements.

To exercise your rights, see our Data Rights process.

14

Consent Withdrawal

Where we rely on consent, you may contact support@marsci-diagnostics.com to request withdrawal of consent. Withdrawal may affect the availability of certain optional features or services.

15

Security Statement

We use administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to help protect information. However, no method of transmission or storage is fully secure, and we do not guarantee absolute security. Some controls are still evolving as the product matures.

16

Children and Minors

MarSci Diagnostics is not intended for children or minors where prohibited by applicable law. If you believe a child has provided personal data to us, contact support@marsci-diagnostics.com.

17

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect product, operational, or legal changes. The latest posted version will control unless applicable law requires otherwise.
